A deadly virus spread to people from the saliva of infected animals. It is also a vaccine-preventable viral disease which occurs in more than 150countries and territories. Dogs are the main source of human rabies death,contributing up to 99% of all rabies transmissions to humans.

Rabies is an infectious viral disease that is almost always fatal followin the onset of clinical symptoms. Domestic dogs are responsible for rabies virus transmission to humans. Yet,rabies can affect both domestic and wild animals. It is spread to people through bites or scratches, usually via saliva.Globally,rabies deaths are rarely reported and children between the ages of 5-14years are frequent victims

Eliminating rabies in dogs

Rabies is a vaccine-preventable disease.Vaccinating dogs is the most cost-effective strategy preventing rabies in people.Dog vaccination reduces deaths attributable to rabies and the need for PEP as a part of dog bite patient care.

Awareness on rabies and preventing dog bites

Education on dog behaviour an bite prevention fot both children and adults is an essential extentsion of a rabies vaccination programme and can decrease both the incidence of human rabies and the financial burden of treating dog bites.Increasing awareness of rabies prevention and control in communites includes education and information on responsible pet ownership ,how to prevent dog bites,and immediate care measures after a bite.Engagement and ownerhip of the programme at the community level increases reach and uptake of key messages.

Symptoms

The average incubation period (time from infection to time of development of symptoms) in humans is 30-60 days, but it may range from less than 10 days to several years.Intial symptoms of rabies include a fever with pain and the unsual or unexplained tingling,pricking,or burning sensation(paraethsia) at the wound site.As a virus spreads to the central nervous system,progressive and fatal inflammation of the brain and spiral code develop.

People may experience:

Pains areas:in the muscles Whole body:dizziness,fatigue,loss of appetite,or malaise Psychological:delirium,fear,or hallucination Gastrointenstinal:nausea or vomiting Muscular:muscle spasms or paralysis with weak muscle Also common:anxiety,brain death,coma,difficulty swallowing,dilated pupil,drooling, excess salivation,headache,mental confusion,seizure,or stiff neck
